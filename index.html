After the Applause

A short film shot on campus after graduation — when the crowds are gone, but something lingers.

This project explores how spaces hold onto moments, even when we move on. It's about the feeling of returning to a place that's no longer the same, yet still familiar.

About This Film

"Some places stay with you, long after you've moved on."

This film captures a moment suspended in time—the quiet aftermath of celebration, when empty spaces hold the echoes of what once was. It's about the relationship between memory and place, how buildings and corridors become repositories of our experiences, keeping them safe long after we've moved on.

In the stillness of an empty campus, we find not absence, but presence— the gentle persistence of memory in the spaces that shaped us.

Why This Film?

"I walked through the campus after everyone had left, and the stillness moved me. It wasn't empty — it was full of echoes. I wanted to document this feeling of endings that don't feel like an end."

Filming Notes:

Location: NYU Abu Dhabi
Season: Summer 2025
Equipment: iPhone 14 Pro
Sound: Anchor - Novo Amor
